About

I graduated from Georgetown University in 2012 with a major in Chinese and a minor in business. Determined to justify my tuition expense and time spent studying Chinese, I applied to a China-based post as a Princeton in Asia fellow after graduation.

During my first year, I served as an English teacher at a university in Wuhan, China. My second post was working at PlaNet Finance China, an international microfinance consultancy. It was during my time there that I was first exposed to the design processes, and its ability to solve problems beyond the aesthetic.

After returning from China, I continued my study of design by taking classes at General Assembly in D.C. and the School of the Art Institute in Chicago. This led me to a UX design position at Morningstar and to eventually heading up the design department at Avant.

I'm interested in bringing the human-centered design process to industries not traditionally affiliated with design. I also believe strongly in the power of conceptual design to create experiences and objects that question convention.
